Seamless connectivity, anywhere with OpenRoaming!

Powered by the industry-standard Passpoint technology, OpenRoaming leverages digital certificates and a standardized authentication process to ensure every connection is secure and quick. No more searching for networks or entering credentials—your device connects automatically, making internet access easier than ever.

OpenRoaming is the result of a powerful collaboration between leading companies like the Wireless Broadband Alliance (WBA), Cisco, Samsung, Google, IronWiFi, and many others. Together, they’re working to create a global standard for Wi-Fi roaming that benefits both users and businesses alike.

How Can IronWiFi's OpenRoaming Help Your Business?

What is OpenRoaming?

OpenRoaming is a wireless roaming solution that allows users to connect to Wi-Fi networks seamlessly and securely, without the need for usernames or passwords. It is a global standard that aims to provide a consistent and secure user experience across different Wi-Fi networks.


OpenRoaming is based on the Passpoint technology, which is an industry-standard that enables seamless and secure Wi-Fi roaming between different networks. Passpoint uses digital certificates and a standardized authentication process to provide a secure and seamless connection experience. With OpenRoaming, users can connect to Wi-Fi networks automatically, without the need to enter credentials or search for available networks.


OpenRoaming is a collaborative effort between the Wireless Broadband Alliance (WBA) and various technology companies, including Cisco, Samsung, Google, IronWiFi, and many others. The goal of OpenRoaming is to provide a consistent and secure user experience across different Wi-Fi networks, regardless of the network operator or service provider. This can be particularly beneficial for businesses that want to provide seamless Wi-Fi connectivity to their customers, employees, or partners.


OpenRoaming is currently available in various locations around the world, including airports, hotels, and other public venues. It is expected to become more widely adopted in the coming years, as more businesses and network operators embrace the benefits of seamless and secure Wi-Fi roaming.
